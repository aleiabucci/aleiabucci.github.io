Research Finding the Story in the Data

Situation

The brief was open-ended: produce thought leadership content on the transformation happening in the US small business market.

Approach

Starting with the market rather than a hypothesis, I conducted extensive secondary research across government data, industry reports, and trade sources, drawing on two years of primary research with small business owners to ground the narrative in real examples. I identified three converging forces to build the story around: an aging owner population, accelerating AI adoption, and the collapse of barriers that had historically made small businesses difficult to serve at scale.

Outcome

A twelve-page market intelligence paper synthesizing more than forty sources into a strategic argument for why the small business technology market represents one of the most significant underappreciated opportunities in venture.

Strategy Mapping & Prioritizing Markets for a Novel Technology

Situation

Our satellite company's subsurface imaging technology had broad potential across any industry that needed to see underground, but no defined primary market.

Approach

Starting with the technology's capabilities rather than a predetermined market, I mapped the full landscape of viable industry applications, evaluating each against commercial factors (market size, willingness to pay, sales cycle, competitive intensity) and technical fit (depth, resolution, and accuracy required).

Outcome

A prioritized market roadmap sequencing entry into geophysical services first, with mining as the beachhead, expanding into water, geothermal, and infrastructure as capabilities scaled. The prioritization shaped the product roadmap, satellite build sequence, fundraising narrative, and early commercial partnerships.

Product From Signal to Product

Situation

The starting point was a broad, open question about what small business owners were currently struggling with.

Approach

I conducted 100 interviews across 12 verticals, then 20 follow-up interviews focused on daily operations. I synthesized the findings into a jobs-to-be-done framework, including a key reframe: the product shouldn't be better documentation, it should be decision support delivered while work is actually happening.

Outcome

A four-day product sprint with engineering and design produced an MVP: an AI-powered operations partner built to capture how a business actually runs and deliver that knowledge to the team in real time.

Operations Building an AI-Native Research Department

Situation

The company was fairly new and moving quickly. Research processes were piecemeal, knowledge lived in disconnected places, and existing structures were borrowed from corporate environments that didn't fit a startup context.

Approach

I mapped out and built a new AI-native research infrastructure from the ground up: ongoing industry intelligence, primary and secondary customer research, a structured ideation process, thought leadership content, and a centralized repository with an AI-powered interface for querying it in real time.

Outcome

What started as a single research role became a fully operational research department, with a consistent shared view of the market, a structured process for evaluating ideas, and institutional knowledge to build on.
